Best Times

The best times to fish can vary depending on the state you live in. In my home state of NC the best times to fish are:

AI Prompt: A man fishing in the spring

  • Spring: Spring is typically considered one of the best times for bass fishing in NC. As the water temperatures start to warm up, bass become more active and move into shallower waters for spawning. Early spring (March to April) can be especially productive as bass are actively feeding before spawning.

AI Prompt: A man fishing in the fall

  • Fall: Fall is another great season for bass fishing in NC. As the water cools down after summer, bass become more active again, feeding heavily to prepare for winter. The fall months, particularly September to November, can offer excellent bass fishing opportunities.

AI Prompt: A man fishing at dawn

  • Early Morning and Dusk: Regardless of the season, early morning and dusk are often the best times of day for bass fishing in NC. Bass are typically more active during low light conditions and may be more willing to strike lures or baits during these times.

How

There is no right or wrong way to fish. But some ways are easier than others. If you're a beginner, I suggest the wacky rig:

  1. Rigging: To set up a wacky rig, you'll need a soft plastic worm or bait of your choice. The most common bait used for a wacky rig is a straight-tail worm, typically around 4 to 6 inches long. You'll also need a wacky rig hook, which is a specialized hook with a circular bend and a weed guard (optional).
  2. Hook Placement: Unlike traditional rigging where the hook is inserted through the bait's head or body, the wacky rig involves hooking the bait through the middle. Simply take your worm or bait and hook it through the center, making sure the hook point is exposed.
  3. Technique: The wacky rig is often used with a finesse fishing approach. Cast your bait near underwater structures, vegetation, or areas where bass are likely to hide. Allow the bait to sink slowly and maintain a subtle twitching or shaking motion to mimic a wounded or struggling baitfish.

Another easy rig to use is the Texas rig:

  1. Rigging: To set up a Texas rig, you'll need a soft plastic bait of your choice, such as a worm, creature bait, or crawfish imitation. You'll also need a bullet or worm weight and an offset worm hook.
  2. Weight Placement: Start by sliding the bullet or worm weight onto your fishing line. The weight can vary in size depending on the depth and structure of the fishing area. Next, tie the offset worm hook to the line using your preferred knot.
  3. Hooking the Bait: Take your soft plastic bait and insert the point of the hook into the head of the bait, then thread it through the body until the hook's bend is fully exposed. You can also use a bait keeper or screw-lock hook for better bait retention.
  4. Technique: The Texas rig is highly versatile and can be used in various fishing environments, including vegetation, rocks, and submerged structures. Cast your rigged bait near potential bass hiding spots and allow it to sink to the desired depth. Use a slow retrieve with occasional pauses and twitches to mimic a natural prey item.
